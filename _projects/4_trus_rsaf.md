We present a novel design framework of synthetic radial aperture focusing for three-dimensional (3D) transrectal ultrasound imaging (**TRUS-rSAF**), in which multiple transmit/receive events at different scanning angles are synthesized to reconstruct a radial plane, securing high spatial resolution and texture uniformity. We derive, for the first time, a **closed-form analytical description** of the method — delineating spatial resolution and grating-lobe positions in the radial dimension — and build an optimization workflow on that foundation. The optimized TRUS-rSAF method improved spatial resolution by up to **50%** over a clinical 3D TRUS reference while providing clinically effective temporal resolution (2–8 volume/sec) with negligible grating-lobe artifacts.
